# Part 1 – Fundamentals & Physics of Thermal Destruction

## 1.1 The Physics of Complete Oxidation
Incineration is the process of breaking down complex organic molecules (sludge oil, plastic, paper) into simple, harmless gasses through high-temperature oxidation.
*   **The Physics:** To achieve complete combustion, the "3 Ts" must be maintained:
    1.  **Temperature:** Must be above **850°C** (for sludge) and up to **1200°C** (for some plastics) to destroy dioxins and furans.
    2.  **Time:** The exhaust gasses must remain in the hot zone for at least **2 seconds** (Residence Time).
    3.  **Turbulence:** The air must be mixed vigorously with the waste to ensure every carbon atom finds an oxygen molecule.

## 1.2 Sludge Oil Combustion Physics
Sludge is a mix of oil, water (up to 50%), and solid particles.
*   **The Problem:** Water doesn't burn; it absorbs heat.
*   **The Physics Solution:** The sludge must be **Pre-heated** (to approx. 90°C) and **Homogenized** (using a high-shear mixer). The "Sludge Burner" then uses high-pressure air or steam to atomize the mixture into a fine mist. The furnace temperature must be high enough to "flash evaporate" the water instantly so the oil can ignite.

## 1.3 Draft and Pressure Physics
The incinerator operates under a **Slight Vacuum (Negative Pressure)**.
*   **The Physics:** An induction fan (ID Fan) at the outlet sucks air through the furnace. This ensures that no toxic gasses or flames leak out into the engine room if a door is opened or a seal fails.

# Part 2 – Major Components & System Layout

## 2.1 The Furnace
*   **Structure:** A double-walled steel chamber. The inner wall is lined with **Refractory** (high-temperature bricks or castable ceramic).
*   **Cooling:** Air is blown between the double walls to keep the outer skin cool enough to touch (Safety requirement).

## 2.2 The Burner Assembly
*   **Pilot Burner:** Burns diesel oil (MGO) to pre-heat the furnace to the required temperature ($600^\circ C – 850^\circ C$).
*   **Main Sludge Burner:** The heavy-duty nozzle that handles the dirty sludge oil.

## 2.3 Sludge Service Tank
*   **Function:** Holds the daily batch of sludge for burning. 
*   **Fittings:** Heating coils and a **Homogenizer / Agitator** to keep the water and oil from separating.

## 2.4 Control and Safety System
*   **Flame Scanner:** Monitors the fire. If the flame goes out, it trips the fuel.
*   **Door Interlock:** Prevents the loading door from being opened if the furnace temperature is too high.

# Part 3 – Normal Operation & Key Parameters

## 3.1 What "Healthy" Looks Like
*   **Exhaust Gas:** Clear and colorless at the funnel. No black smoke.
*   **Furnace Pressure:** Steady negative pressure (e.g., -5 to -10 mmWG).
*   **Sludge Flame:** Stable, bright orange/yellow.

## 3.2 Typical Operating Parameters

| Parameter | Healthy Value | Alarm / Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Furnace Temp** | 850°C – 1100°C | < 850°C (Illegal Burn) |
| **Flue Gas Temp** | 250°C – 350°C | > 450°C (High Temp Trip) |
| **Furnace Pressure** | -10 mmWG | > 0 mmWG (Positive Press) |
| **Sludge Tank Temp** | 80°C – 95°C | < 70°C (Poor Atomization) |

# Part 4 – Common Faults & Root Causes

## 4.1 "Flame Failure" during Sludge Burning
*   **Symptom:** Incinerator stops; alarm active.
*   **Root Cause:** Too much water in the sludge or a blocked sludge nozzle.
*   **Physics:** If the sludge contains a "Slug" of 100% water, it will extinguish the flame instantly.

## 4.2 Furnace Positive Pressure (The "Puff")
*   **Symptom:** Smoke leaking from the incinerator casing into the engine room.
*   **Root Cause:** Failed ID fan or a blocked "Spark Arrester" at the funnel.
*   **Danger:** Toxic gasses ($CO, SO_x$) entering the machinery space.

## 4.3 Refractory Damage (Cracking)
*   **Symptom:** Outer casing of the incinerator is glowing red or "blistering."
*   **Root Cause:** Rapid cooling or mechanical impact from loading heavy trash.
*   **Physics:** Thermal shock causes the bricks to expand and contract at different rates, leading to "Spalling."

# Part 5 – Expert Troubleshooting & Trade Tricks

## 5.1 The "Water-Drain" Trick for Sludge
*   **Trade Trick:** Before starting the incinerator, let the sludge tank sit for 4 hours with the heater ON and the agitator OFF. **Drain the bottom water to the bilge tank until you see oil.** This removes the "slugs" of water that cause flame failure.

## 5.2 Cleaning the ID Fan "In-Situ"
*   **Expert Insight:** ID fans accumulate soot on the blades, causing unbalance and vibration. **Trade Trick:** While the fan is running at low speed, spray a small amount of fresh water into the fan inlet. The water droplets act like tiny hammers, knocking the soot off the blades.

## 5.3 Detecting a Blocked "Scavenge Air" Port
*   **Trade Trick:** If the burner flame is "lazy" and dark, the scavenge air holes around the nozzle are blocked with carbon. Clean them with a small drill bit. **This restores the "Turbulence" required for a clean burn.**

## 5.4 Using the "Optical Scanner" Test
*   **Trade Trick:** To test the flame scanner, pull it out of its holder while the burner is running. The incinerator should trip within **4 seconds**. If not, the safety logic is bypassed or faulty.

# Part 6 – Maintenance & Preventive Checks

## 6.1 Daily Waste Log
*   **Critical Action:** Every operation must be recorded in the **Garbage Record Book**. You must record the Start/Stop times, the quantity of sludge burned, and the quantity of ash produced.

## 6.2 Monthly Refractory Patching
*   **Method:** Inspect the internal bricks. Use "Plastic Refractory" (moldable clay) to fill any small cracks or holes. This prevents the heat from reaching the outer steel shell.

## 6.3 Burner Nozzle Cleaning
*   **Method:** Clean the sludge nozzle in a sonic bath or with a wooden pick. **Never use a steel wire**, as it will enlarge the orifice and destroy the spray pattern.

# Part 7 – Miscellaneous Knowledge

*   **Prohibited Items:** Under MARPOL Annex VI, you are **strictly prohibited** from burning:
    1.  PCB-containing materials.
    2.  Garbage containing more than a trace of heavy metals.
    3.  Refined petroleum products containing halogen compounds.
    4.  Exhaust gas cleaning system residues (Scrubber sludge).
*   **ECA Zones:** Some Emission Control Areas (ECAs) prohibit the use of incinerators while in port or within a certain distance from land.
