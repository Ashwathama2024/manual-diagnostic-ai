# Part 1 – Fundamentals & Physics of Waste Reduction

## 1.1 The Physics of Volume Reduction
MARPOL Annex V strictly limits the discharge of garbage at sea. Compaction is the primary method for managing the massive volume of waste generated by the crew.
*   **The Physics:** Compaction works by reducing the "Void Space" between garbage particles using high mechanical force.
*   **Compaction Ratio:** Most marine compactors achieve a ratio of **5:1 or 10:1**. This means 10 cubic meters of loose trash is squeezed into 1 cubic meter, significantly increasing the storage capacity of the ship's garbage lockers.

## 1.2 Hydraulic Force Physics
The compactor uses a hydraulic ram to crush the waste.
*   **The Physics:** Force $F = P \cdot A$. 
    A small electric motor drives a high-pressure pump (e.g., 200 bar) acting on a large diameter piston. This creates tens of tons of crushing force.
*   **Elasticity vs. Plasticity:** Compaction is only effective if the material reaches its "Plastic State" (permanent deformation). Materials like springy plastics or rubber may "bounce back" if the ram is retracted too quickly.

## 1.3 MARPOL Annex V Compliance (The Regulatory Physics)
*   **Plastic:** Is **NEVER** allowed to be discharged at sea. It must be compacted and stored for shore disposal.
*   **Food Waste:** Can be discharged (if macerated to < 25mm) at specific distances from land.
*   **Mixed Waste:** If garbage is mixed with prohibited items (like plastic), the whole batch must be treated as prohibited.

# Part 2 – Major Components & System Layout

## 2.1 The Crushing Chamber
A heavy-duty reinforced steel box where the garbage is loaded.
*   **Design:** Often features "Teeth" or "Spikes" on the ram face to puncture bags and break glass or wood.

## 2.2 Hydraulic Power Unit (HPU)
*   **Components:** Reservoir, pump, and control valves.
*   **Safe Fluid:** Usually uses biodegradable hydraulic oil to prevent environmental contamination if a hose bursts.

## 2.3 Compactor Bags / Containers
Specialized high-strength plastic or woven bags that hold the compressed "Bale." 
*   **Physics:** The bale is tied under pressure to ensure it doesn't expand when the ram is lifted.

## 2.4 Safety Interlocks (Crucial)
*   **Door Switch:** Prevents the ram from moving if the loading door is open.
*   **Emergency Stop:** Instantly cuts power and vents hydraulic pressure.

# Part 3 – Normal Operation & Key Parameters

## 3.1 What "Healthy" Looks Like
*   **Cycle Time:** The ram should move down and back within 30–60 seconds.
*   **Sound:** A smooth "Hum" from the pump. A "Squeal" indicates the relief valve is lifting (overload).
*   **Bale Quality:** Solid, rectangular, and easy to handle with a pallet jack.

## 3.2 Typical Operating Parameters

| Parameter | Healthy Value | Alarm / Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hydraulic Pressure** | 150 – 210 bar | > 250 bar (Relief Valve) |
| **Compaction Force** | 10 – 50 Tons | Depends on unit size |
| **Motor Current** | < Rated Amps | High Amps (Mechanical Jam) |
| **Oil Temp** | 30°C – 50°C | > 65°C (Cooler Needed) |

# Part 4 – Common Faults & Root Causes

## 4.1 "Spring-Back" (The Bale Expands)
*   **Symptom:** Garbage expands to original size when the door is opened.
*   **Root Cause:** Compacting too much "Springy" plastic or not holding the ram at the bottom of the stroke (Dwell Time).
*   **Solution:** Use the "Hold" function for 10 seconds at maximum pressure to ensure permanent deformation.

## 4.2 Hydraulic Ram Creep
*   **Symptom:** The ram slowly sinks down when the machine is OFF.
*   **Root Cause:** Internal leak in the hydraulic cylinder seals or a leaking solenoid valve.

## 4.3 Mechanical Jam (The "Clog")
*   **Symptom:** Motor runs but ram doesn't move; loud "Squealing" noise.
*   **Root Cause:** A heavy metal object (like a shackle or tool) accidentally thrown in the trash.

# Part 5 – Expert Troubleshooting & Trade Tricks

## 5.1 The "Cardboard Sandwich" Trick
*   **Trade Trick:** To get a solid, stable bale of loose plastic or paper, place a thick sheet of corrugated cardboard at the **bottom** and the **top** of the bale. The cardboard provides structure and prevents the bale ties from cutting through the plastic.

## 5.2 Detecting Leaking Seals via "Temperature"
*   **Trade Trick:** If the compactor is slow, touch the hydraulic cylinder. **If one side of the cylinder is significantly hotter than the other**, the internal piston seal is leaking. The high-pressure oil is "bypassing" the piston and generating heat through friction.

## 5.3 Managing "Leachate" (Liquid Waste)
*   **Expert Insight:** Compacting wet trash produces "Leachate" (dirty water). **Trade Trick:** Ensure the compactor's drain plug is connected to the **Grey Water** or **Sewage** system. If leachate spills on the deck, it will attract flies and create a biohazard.

## 5.4 Using the "Auto-Cycle" Correctly
*   **Expert Insight:** Most modern compactors have an "Auto-Cycle" button. **Trade Trick:** Always stay near the machine during the first cycle of a new bale. If you hear "Cracking" or "Popping" sounds, a rigid object is likely damaging the floor plate.

# Part 6 – Maintenance & Preventive Checks

## 6.1 Weekly Routine
*   **Greasing:** Grease the ram guide rails. Friction here can waste 20% of the crushing force.
*   **Cleaning:** Wash the crushing chamber with a mild disinfectant to prevent odors.

## 6.2 Annual Hydraulic Service
*   **Method:** Change the hydraulic oil and filter. **Check the hose integrity.** Hydraulic hoses on deck are exposed to UV light and salt, which causes "Dry Rot." Replace any hose showing cracks.

## 6.3 Garbage Record Book (GRB)
*   **Critical Action:** Every bale produced and every bale landed ashore must be recorded in the **GRB Part 1**. **Check the Weights:** Ensure the "Volume Discharged" recorded matches the "Receipt" provided by the shore facility.

# Part 7 – Miscellaneous Knowledge

*   **Hazardous Waste:** Never compact batteries, aerosol cans, or paint tins. **Explosion Risk:** An aerosol can under 20 tons of pressure is a grenade.
*   **Glass Crusher:** Some compactors include a "Glass Crusher" attachment. Glass must be crushed into small "Cullet" (< 25mm) before being bagged for shore disposal or (in rare cases) legal discharge.
