# Part 1 – Fundamentals & Physics of Respiratory Protection

## 1.1 The Physics of Positive Pressure
Modern marine SCBA are "Positive Pressure" systems.
*   **The Physics:** The pressure inside the mask is always slightly higher than the outside atmospheric pressure (approx. +3 mbar).
*   **Safety Advantage:** If the mask seal is slightly broken (e.g., by a beard or a bump), air will leak **out**, but no toxic gas or smoke can leak **in**. 

## 1.2 The Physics of Compressed Air Supply
*   **Boyle’s Law:** $P_1 V_1 = P_2 V_2$. 
    A standard 6-liter cylinder charged to 200 bar contains **1,200 liters** of air (expanded to atmospheric pressure).
*   **Consumption Physics:** The average human consumes approx. **40 liters of air per minute** during moderate work. 
*   **Duration Calculation:** 1,200 liters / 40 lpm = **30 minutes total duration**. 
    **Safety Margin:** You must subtract the "Safety Reserve" (Whistle time), leaving approx. 20–25 minutes of actual work time.

## 1.3 Thermodynamics of Air Expansion
*   **The Physics:** As high-pressure air (200 bar) expands through the 1st stage regulator to the lung-demand valve (7 bar), its temperature drops. 
*   **Problem:** If the air contains moisture, it can freeze the regulator (Icing). **Requirement:** SCBA air must be **extra dry** (Grade D or higher).

# Part 2 – Major Components & System Layout

## 2.1 Compressed Air Cylinder
*   **Material:** Steel or Carbon Fiber (Composite). Carbon fiber is 50% lighter, reducing crew fatigue.
*   **Cylinder Valve:** Features a ratchet lock to prevent accidental closing.

## 2.2 First Stage Regulator (Pressure Reducer)
Reduces the 200/300 bar cylinder pressure down to a stable medium pressure (approx. 6–9 bar).

## 2.3 Lung Demand Valve (LDV) / Second Stage
*   **Function:** Delivers air only when the wearer inhales. 
*   **Physics:** Uses a sensitive diaphragm to match the wearer's breath.

## 2.4 The Full-Face Mask
*   **Material:** Silicone or EPDM rubber. 
*   **Physics:** Features a "Wide-vision" visor and a "Speech Diaphragm" to allow communication without removing the mask.

## 2.5 Low Pressure Warning (The Whistle)
A mechanical whistle that triggers when the cylinder pressure drops below **50–60 bar**. **Physics:** Warns the wearer they have approx. **10 minutes** of air left to reach safety.

# Part 3 – Normal Operation & Key Parameters

## 3.1 What "Healthy" Looks Like
*   **Mask Seal:** No "Hissing" sound when the wearer holds their breath.
*   **Whistle:** Loud and clear during the "Bleed-down" test.
*   **Gauge:** Pressure matches the bottle status (Green zone).

## 3.2 Typical Operating Parameters

| Parameter | Healthy Value | Alarm / Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cylinder Pressure** | 180 – 300 bar | < 180 bar (Refill) |
| **Warning Whistle** | 55 bar ± 5 | < 50 bar (Critical) |
| **Leakage Rate** | < 10 bar in 1 min | > 20 bar (Repair Req.) |
| **Air Quality** | Odorless / Dry | Oil Smell (Stop) |

# Part 4 – Common Faults & Root Causes

## 4.1 Mask Leakage (The #1 Safety Risk)
*   **Symptom:** Air constantly venting from the mask or "Fogging" of the visor.
*   **Root Cause:** Improperly adjusted head straps or the wearer has facial hair.
*   **Danger:** High air consumption leads to "Running out of air" much faster than calculated.

## 4.2 LDV "Free-Flow"
*   **Symptom:** Air bursts out of the mask uncontrollably.
*   **Root Cause:** Dirt in the demand valve or the "Positive Pressure" button is engaged without the mask being on the face.

## 4.3 Gauge Discrepancy
*   **Symptom:** The mechanical gauge on the harness doesn't match the one on the cylinder.
*   **Root Cause:** Blocked capillary tube or a damaged gauge face.

# Part 5 – Expert Troubleshooting & Trade Tricks

## 5.1 The "Negative Pressure" Seal Test
*   **Trade Trick:** Before opening the cylinder valve, put on the mask and inhale deeply while blocking the LDV inlet with your hand. **The mask should "Suck" onto your face and stay there.** If it falls off, your seal is bad.

## 5.2 The "Soap Bubble" Cylinder Test
*   **Trade Trick:** If a cylinder is losing 10 bar overnight, the valve gland is leaking. **Method:** Dip the cylinder valve in a bucket of water or use soapy water. **Expert Insight:** 90% of SCBA leaks are at the **Bursting Disc** or the valve thread.

## 5.3 Managing "Fogging" (The Dish Soap Trick)
*   **Trade Trick:** visors fog up due to moisture in the breath. **Expert Insight:** Wipe the *inside* of the visor with a tiny drop of liquid dish soap and buff it clear. This creates a surfactant layer that prevents water droplets from forming.

## 5.4 High-Pressure "Creep" Detection
*   **Expert Insight:** If the air smells like "Burnt Rubber," the first-stage regulator is failing and allowing high-pressure air to "Creep" into the low-pressure hose. **Action:** Stop using immediately; the low-pressure hose could explode.

# Part 6 – Maintenance & Preventive Checks

## 6.1 Weekly Routine
*   **Visual:** Check the cylinder pressure. Top up if < 180 bar.
*   **Test:** Perform the "High-Pressure Leak Test" (Open valve, check gauge, close valve, wait 1 min).

## 6.2 Monthly Sanitation
*   **Method:** Wash the face mask in a disinfecting solution (e.g., Virkon or mild soap). **Warning:** Ensure the LDV is dried thoroughly with compressed air to prevent internal mold.

## 6.3 Annual Inspection (Mandatory)
*   **Requirement:** An authorized service technician must perform a "Flow Test" on a computerized test bench (e.g., Dräger Testor). They will verify the breathing resistance and the whistle trip point.

## 6.4 5-Year Hydro-test
*   **Requirement:** All SCBA cylinders must be hydro-statically tested every 5 years to verify their structural integrity. **Marking:** Check the "Date Stamp" on the cylinder neck.

# Part 7 – Miscellaneous Knowledge

*   **EEBD vs. SCBA:** An EEBD (Emergency Escape Breathing Device) is for **Escape only** (approx. 10 mins air). A SCBA is for **Working/Firefighting** (approx. 30+ mins air). **Safety Rule:** Never use an EEBD to fight a fire.
*   **Compressor Maintenance:** The SCBA filling compressor must have its air quality tested every 6 months for **CO, CO2, and Oil content.**
