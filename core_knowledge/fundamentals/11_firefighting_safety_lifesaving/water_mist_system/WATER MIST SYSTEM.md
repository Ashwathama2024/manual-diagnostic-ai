# Part 1 – Fundamentals & Physics of Water Mist

## 1.1 The Physics of Evaporative Cooling
Water mist extinguishes fire differently than a standard sprinkler.
*   **The Physics:** By forcing water through specialized nozzles at high pressure (e.g., 60–140 bar), the water is broken into microscopic droplets (approx. 50–100 microns).
*   **Surface Area:** The surface area of 1 liter of water mist is **100 times greater** than 1 liter from a standard nozzle. 
*   **Heat Absorption:** This massive surface area allows for instantaneous evaporation. As the water turns to steam, it absorbs a huge amount of **Latent Heat**, cooling the fire below its ignition point.

## 1.2 The Physics of Inerting (Oxygen Displacement)
*   **The Process:** When the microscopic water droplets evaporate into steam, the volume expands by approx. **1600 times**. 
*   **Localized Inerting:** This expansion physically displaces the Oxygen $(\text{O}_2)$ surrounding the fire, effectively "suffocating" it at the source without flooding the entire room with gas.

## 1.3 Smoke Scrubbing Physics
*   **The Physics:** The fine mist droplets bind with smoke and soot particles in the air. 
*   **Effect:** This "scrubs" the atmosphere, improving visibility for the fire crew and absorbing toxic gasses ($CO, SO_x$).

# Part 2 – Major Components & System Layout

## 2.1 High-Pressure Pump Unit
A set of vertical multi-stage centrifugal or piston pumps.
*   **Starting:** Automatic start on signal from the fire detection system.
*   **Water Supply:** Usually connected to the ship's Fresh Water tank (to prevent nozzle clogging) with a back-up from the Fire Main (Seawater).

## 2.2 Section Valves (Solenoid Controlled)
Located outside the protected space. Each "Section" covers one specific high-risk area (e.g., Main Engine, Generator 1, Purifier Room).

## 2.3 Water Mist Nozzles
Specialized stainless steel nozzles with very small orifices and internal "Whirl" chambers to create the fine mist.

## 2.4 Control Panel and Detectors
*   **Logic:** Uses "Flame Detectors" (UV/IR) for instant response. **Physics:** The system is designed for "Local Application," meaning it targets only the machine that is on fire.

# Part 4 – Common Faults & Root Causes

## 4.1 Nozzle Blockage (The #1 Maintenance Issue)
*   **Symptom:** Water "Streams" out instead of a fine mist; fire is not extinguished.
*   **Root Cause:** Rust scales from the pipes or salt crystals (if seawater was used for testing).
*   **Physics:** Because the nozzle orifices are < 1mm, even a single grain of sand will destroy the atomization pattern.

## 4.2 Flame Detector False Alarms
*   **Symptom:** System discharges randomly on a specific engine.
*   **Root Cause:** "Flicker" from a loose reflective surface or hot exhaust gasses.
*   **Expert Insight:** UV/IR detectors can be "blinded" by oily soot on the lens.

## 4.3 Jockey Pump "Short-Cycling"
*   **Symptom:** Small maintenance pump starts/stops every few minutes.
*   **Root Cause:** A tiny leak in a section valve or a weeping nozzle joint.

# Part 5 – Expert Troubleshooting & Trade Tricks

## 5.1 The "Piece of Paper" Atomization Test
*   **Trade Trick:** During the annual air-blow test, hold a piece of paper 50cm from the nozzle. **Expert Insight:** If the paper gets a "Wet Spot" in the center, the nozzle is blocked and "Streaming." If the paper gets evenly damp across the surface, the atomization is perfect.

## 5.2 Resetting a "Tripped" Flame Detector
*   **Trade Trick:** If a detector is causing false alarms, check for **High Vibration**. **Method:** Mount the detector on a rubber "Vibration Isolator." Excessive shaking can cause the internal sensor elements to "flicker" electrically, mimicking a fire.

## 5.3 Clearing a Blocked Nozzle "In-Situ"
*   **Expert Insight:** Do not stick a wire into the nozzle! **Trade Trick:** Remove the nozzle and use compressed air to blow *backwards* through the tip while soaking it in a weak acid (Citric Acid) to dissolve any calcium scale.

## 5.4 Using the "Manual Release" Correctly
*   **Critical Knowledge:** Every system has a manual pull-handle at the pump unit. **Trade Trick:** Always verify which section valve is being opened. If you open the "Purifier" section for a "Main Engine" fire, you are just flooding the floor with water while the engine burns.

# Part 6 – Maintenance & Preventive Checks

## 6.1 Weekly Routine
*   **Level Check:** Ensure the Fresh Water supply tank is topped up.
*   **Visual:** Inspect nozzles for "Paint" or "Plastic bags" left over from maintenance.

## 6.2 Annual Air Test (Mandatory)
*   **Method:** Close the water supply and connect a high-pressure air bottle to the manifold. Verify air discharge from every single nozzle. This is the only way to ensure the pipes aren't blocked by rust.

## 6.3 Pump Overhaul (5-Year)
*   **Method:** High-pressure piston pumps use specialized ceramic plungers. **Check:** Look for "Scoring" on the plungers. Even a hairline scratch will cause the pump pressure to drop by 20 bar.

# Part 7 – Miscellaneous Knowledge

*   **Hyper-Mist vs. CO2:** The biggest advantage of Water Mist is that **it is safe for humans.** You can discharge it while the crew is still in the room. It cools the atmosphere and doesn't remove the oxygen from the whole room.
*   **Galley Protection:** Many ships use a smaller version of the water mist system for the "Deep Fat Fryer" in the galley.
