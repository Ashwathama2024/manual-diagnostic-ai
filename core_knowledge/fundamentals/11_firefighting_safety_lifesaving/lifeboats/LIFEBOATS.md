# Part 1 – Fundamentals & Physics of Survival Craft

## 1.1 The Physics of Launching (Gravity Davits)
A lifeboat must be launchable even if the ship has no power.
*   **The Physics:** Most lifeboats use **Gravity Davits**. By releasing the brake, the weight of the boat $(m \cdot g)$ is used to swing the davit arms out and lower the boat to the water.
*   **List and Trim Physics:** SOLAS requires that the boat must be launchable even if the ship is listing at **20°** to either side and trimmed **10°** by head or stern.

## 1.2 The Physics of Buoyancy and Self-Righting
*   **Self-Righting:** A totally enclosed lifeboat is designed to be "Self-Righting." 
*   **The Physics:** The center of gravity $(G)$ is kept very low (engine and fuel at the bottom), while the cabin top has a high volume of air. If the boat capsizes, the high Center of Buoyancy $(B)$ creates a massive "Righting Lever" $(GZ)$ that flips the boat upright instantly.

## 1.3 On-Load vs. Off-Load Release Physics
The release hooks are the most dangerous part of the lifeboat.
*   **Off-Load Release:** The hooks can only be opened when the boat is in the water and the weight is off the wires.
*   **On-Load Release:** Allows the hooks to be opened while the boat is still suspended. **Physics:** This is necessary in high seas to prevent the boat from being smashed against the hull as the ship rolls. It uses a "Hydrostatic Interlock" to prevent accidental release.

# Part 2 – Major Components & System Layout

## 2.1 The Boat Structure
*   **Hull:** Fire-retardant GRP (Glass Reinforced Plastic) with a double-hull design filled with polyurethane foam for "unsinkability."
*   **Water Spray System:** A pump that sprays seawater over the entire exterior to protect the crew from fire while rowing through burning oil.

## 2.2 The Lifeboat Engine
Usually a small, multi-cylinder compression-ignition diesel engine (e.g., Bukh, Lister Petter).
*   **Starting:** Dual independent starting systems (Electric + Manual/Hydraulic). 
*   **Cooling:** Closed-loop (keel cooling) to allow the engine to run while the boat is in the davit for testing.

## 2.3 Compressed Air System
Contains bottles of air to provide positive pressure inside the cabin for **at least 10 minutes** while passing through smoke or fire.

## 2.4 The Davit Winch
A mechanical winch with a centrifugal brake to control the lowering speed (approx. 0.5 to 1.0 m/s).

# Part 3 – Normal Operation & Key Parameters

## 3.1 What "Healthy" Looks Like
*   **Engine:** Starts within 2 seconds. No smoke from the exhaust.
*   **Hooks:** "Release Handle" is in the locked position with the safety pin inserted. The indicator shows **Green**.
*   **Battery:** Charging status is "Normal" on the LSA battery charger.

## 3.2 Typical Operating Parameters

| Parameter | Healthy Value | Alarm / Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Engine Speed** | 2500 – 3000 RPM | < 2000 (Low Power) |
| **Lowering Speed** | 0.6 – 1.0 m / sec | > 1.3 m / sec (Brake Fail) |
| **Air Bottle Press** | 200 bar | < 180 bar (Refill) |
| **Battery Voltage** | 12.5 V – 14.0 V | < 11.5 V (Start Fail) |

# Part 4 – Common Faults & Root Causes

## 4.1 "Release Hook" Seizure
*   **Symptom:** Release handle is stuck or requires excessive force.
*   **Root Cause:** Corrosion of the internal cam mechanism due to lack of grease.
*   **Danger:** A stuck hook can cause the boat to hang by one end, leading to a catastrophic fall.

## 4.2 Engine "Hydraulic Lock"
*   **Symptom:** Engine won't turn over; starter solenoid clicks.
*   **Root Cause:** Water or fuel has leaked into the cylinder while the boat was sitting in the davit.

## 4.3 Davit Brake "Slipping"
*   **Symptom:** The boat "jerks" or creeps down while in the stowed position.
*   **Root Cause:** Oil leak from the gearbox onto the centrifugal brake pads.

# Part 5 – Expert Troubleshooting & Trade Tricks

## 5.1 The "Hook Indicator" Verification
*   **Trade Trick:** Never trust the "Green Color" on the hook indicator alone. **Expert Insight:** Physically look at the **Release Cam**. If the cam is not 100% engaged with the hook tail, the hook can release under vibration. **Trade Trick:** Wipe the cam with a white rag; if there's no fresh grease, the mechanism is likely seized.

## 5.2 Starting a "Cold" Lifeboat Engine
*   **Trade Trick:** If the engine won't start in winter, use the "Ether" (Starting fluid) port if fitted. **Expert Insight:** Many Bukh engines have a "Cold Start" button on the fuel pump. You must physically push this button *before* turning the key to increase the fuel rack position.

## 5.3 Clearing an Air-locked Fuel System
*   **Trade Trick:** Lifeboat fuel tanks are low. If you run out of fuel, gravity priming is slow. **Trade Trick:** Blow a tiny amount of compressed air into the fuel tank vent while cracking the injector lines to force the fuel through the system.

## 5.4 The "Hydrostatic Release" Override
*   **Critical Knowledge:** If you are in the water and the hooks won't release because the "Hydrostatic" unit is stuck, you must use the **Emergency Release (The "Break-Glass" lever)**. **Warning:** This bypasses all safety interlocks. Only use it when the boat is 100% in the water.

# Part 6 – Maintenance & Preventive Checks

## 6.1 Weekly Routine (Mandatory)
*   **Engine Run:** Run the engine for at least 3 minutes. Test the ahead and astern gears.
*   **Visual:** Check the condition of the "Falls" (Wire ropes). Look for rust or "Kinks."

## 6.2 Quarterly Davit Maintenance
*   **Method:** Lower the boat to the embarkation deck. Grease the davit sheaves and the winch gears. **Brake Test:** Verify the "Stop" function of the hand brake.

## 6.3 Annual and 5-Year Inspection
*   **Requirement:** An authorized service provider must perform a thorough examination of the hooks and davits. 
*   **5-Year Load Test:** The boat must be weight-tested at **110% of its full load** (including crew weight) to ensure the structure and winches are sound.

# Part 7 – Miscellaneous Knowledge

*   **Free-Fall Lifeboat:** A specialized boat that slides off a ramp at the stern. **Physics:** Uses kinetic energy to launch far away from the ship. It requires specialized "Seating Positions" to prevent spinal injuries.
*   **Painters:** The "Painter" is the rope that holds the boat to the ship after launch. **Safety Rule:** Always ensure the painter is secured *forward* of the lifeboat before launching.
