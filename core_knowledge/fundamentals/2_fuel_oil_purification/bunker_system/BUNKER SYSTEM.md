# Part 1 – Fundamentals & Physics of Bunkering

## 1.1 Coriolis Mass Flow Physics (The Gold Standard)
Modern bunkering relies on Coriolis meters to prevent the "Cappuccino Effect" (aerated fuel).
*   **The Physics:** A Coriolis meter consists of a vibrating tube. When fluid flows through it, the tube twists due to the **Coriolis Acceleration**. 
*   **Mass Measurement:** Unlike volume meters, the Coriolis twist is directly proportional to the **Mass Flow Rate** ($\dot{m}$), independent of temperature, viscosity, or air bubbles. This is critical for avoiding disputes between the ship and the bunker barge.

## 1.2 The Physics of Fuel Incompatibility
Bunker fuels from different sources often use different refining processes (e.g., Paraffinic vs. Aromatic).
*   **The Chemistry:** Mixing two stable fuels can cause **Asphaltene Precipitation**. 
*   **Physics of Sludging:** Asphaltenes are kept in suspension by "Resins." If the resin balance is disturbed by a different fuel, the asphaltenes "drop out," creating a thick, tar-like sludge that can block filters and seize purifiers in minutes.

## 1.3 MARPOL Sampling Physics
MARPOL Annex VI requires a representative sample of the fuel delivered.
*   **The Physics of the Drip Sampler:** A continuous "drip" must be taken from the manifold throughout the *entire* bunkering period. This ensures the sample is a "homogenous" representation of the total batch, not just a snapshot from the beginning or end.

# Part 2 – Major Components & System Layout

## 2.1 Bunker Manifold and Sampling Point
*   **Manifold:** The connection point on deck for the barge hose. Equipped with pressure gauges and a temperature sensor.
*   **Sampler:** A manual or automatic "Drip Sampler" (MARPOL Line Sampler) located at the manifold.

## 2.2 Storage Tanks (Double Bottom / Wing Tanks)
*   **Structure:** Located within the ship's hull. Modern ships use "Protected Fuel Tanks" (not in direct contact with the outer shell).
*   **Venting:** Each tank has an "Air Pipe" with a flame screen and a high-high level alarm (95% / 98%) to prevent overflows.

## 2.3 Transfer Pumps
*   **Type:** High-capacity positive displacement pumps (Screw or Gear type).
*   **Function:** Moves fuel from storage tanks to the Settling Tank for purification.

## 2.4 Tank Sounding System
*   **Manual Sounding:** The "Steel Tape" method (the ultimate truth).
*   **Remote Gauging:** Uses radar, ultrasonic, or hydrostatic pressure sensors to monitor tank levels in the ECR.

# Part 3 – Normal Operation & Key Parameters

## 3.1 What "Healthy" Looks Like
*   **Bunker Plan:** A clear plan showing which tanks will be filled and in what order (never mix new fuel with old if possible).
*   **Flow Rate:** Within the rated capacity of the ship's piping to avoid "Pressure Surges."
*   **Communication:** Constant radio contact between the manifold watchkeeper and the bunker barge.

## 3.2 Typical Operating Parameters

| Parameter | Healthy Value | Alarm / Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bunker Temperature** | 30°C – 45°C | > 60°C (Flashpoint Risk) |
| **Pumping Pressure** | 2.0 – 5.0 bar | > 7.0 bar (Burst Risk) |
| **Tank Level (Final)** | 85% – 90% | > 95% (High Alarm) |
| **Sounding Tape Status** | Clean and dry | "Water Cut" (Water in Fuel) |
| **Flow Meter Error** | < 0.5% | High Deviation |

# Part 4 – Common Faults & Root Causes

## 4.1 The "Cappuccino Effect" (Air in Fuel)
*   **Symptom:** The barge meter shows more fuel delivered than the ship's soundings indicate; frothy fuel at the manifold.
*   **Root Cause:** Barge injecting air into the fuel line to increase volume.
*   **Physics:** Air bubbles increase the volume ($V$) without increasing the mass ($m$). Coriolis meters detect this; traditional volume meters do not.

## 4.2 Tank Overflow (The Oil Spill)
*   **Symptom:** Fuel coming out of the air pipe / vent.
*   **Root Cause:** Blocked vent pipe, faulty high-level alarm, or poor communication during the "top-off" stage.
*   **Critical Action:** Stop bunkering immediately and activate **SOPEP (Shipboard Oil Pollution Emergency Plan).**

## 4.3 High "Cat Fines" / Water Content
*   **Symptom:** Oil analysis report (e.g., FOBAS/VISWA) shows values exceeding ISO 8217.
*   **Root Cause:** Contaminated fuel from the refinery or barge tanks.

# Part 5 – Expert Troubleshooting & Trade Tricks

## 5.1 The "Spot Test" for Compatibility
*   **Trade Trick:** Before mixing new fuel with old in a tank, perform a "Spot Test." 
    *   **Method:** Mix 50/50 of the two fuels and place a drop on filter paper. 
    *   **Result:** A clean, uniform spot = Compatible. A spot with a dark "ring" in the center = **Incompatible (Danger).**

## 5.2 Calculating "Weight-in-Air"
*   **Expert Insight:** Bunkers are sold by the **Metric Ton (Mass)**. You must convert Volume to Mass using the density corrected for temperature. 
    \[ m = V \cdot \rho_{15} \cdot [1 - 0.00065(T - 15)] \]
    Always use the density provided in the **BDN (Bunker Delivery Note).**

## 5.3 Manual Sounding (The "Water Cut" Paste)
*   **Trade Trick:** Apply "Water Finding Paste" to the bottom of the sounding tape. If the paste turns purple/red, you have water at the bottom of your bunker tank.

## 5.4 The "Y-Strainer" Check
*   **Trade Trick:** There is often a coarse strainer at the bunker manifold. If the bunkering pressure is high but the flow is low, the strainer is likely blocked with "Rags" or plastic from the barge hoses.

# Part 6 – Maintenance & Preventive Checks

## 6.1 Pre-Bunker Checklist
*   **SOPEP Gear:** Ensure sawdust, scupper plugs, and absorbent pads are on deck.
*   **Communication:** Confirm the "Stop" signal with the barge.
*   **Valves:** Ensure all tank valves are correctly set (Double-check "Blind Flanges").

## 6.2 Tank Inspection
*   **Heating Coils:** Pressure test heating coils annually to ensure no oil is leaking into the steam/condensate system.
*   **Alarms:** Test the 95% and 98% alarms before every bunkering operation.

## 6.3 MARPOL Sample Storage
*   **Requirement:** Retain the MARPOL sample for at least 12 months or until the fuel is consumed. Keep it in a cool, dark locker.

# Part 7 – Miscellaneous Knowledge

*   **BDN (Bunker Delivery Note):** A legal document. Ensure the "Sulphur Content" listed matches the ship's area of operation (e.g., 0.1% for ECAs, 0.5% Global).
*   **Fuel Stabilizers:** Chemical additives that can be added to storage tanks to help prevent asphaltene fallout if compatibility is borderline.
