# Part 1 – Fundamentals & Physics of Lube Oil Separation

## 1.1 The Role of the Purifier in Tribology
The lube oil (LO) purifier is the only means of continuously cleaning the engine's "lifeblood" during operation. Unlike fuel which is used once, LO is recirculated thousands of times.
*   **The Physics of Contamination:** Lube oil accumulates carbon (soot), metal wear particles (Fe, Cu, Sn), and water (condensate or leakage). 
*   **Separation Physics:** Because the density difference between lube oil ($\approx 0.90$) and water ($1.00$) is smaller than with fuel, the separation requires even more precise control of temperature and flow.

## 1.2 TBN and Additive Preservation Physics
*   **TBN (Total Base Number):** Modern LO contains alkaline additives to neutralize acids formed during combustion. 
*   **The Physics of Over-Purification:** Excessive purification (too high temperature or excessive water washing) can actually "strip" the useful additives from the oil. The goal is to remove contaminants without affecting the chemical package.

## 1.3 Water Removal Physics
Water in LO is catastrophic. It causes:
1.  **Emulsification:** The oil becomes "milky," losing its load-carrying capacity.
2.  **Corrosion:** Acidic water attacks bearing surfaces (pitting).
3.  **Hydrolysis:** Water breaks down the oil's chemical additives.

# Part 2 – Major Components & System Layout

## 2.1 The Purifier Unit
Mechanically identical to the Fuel Oil Purifier (Bowl, Disc Stack, Sliding Bottom), but often smaller in capacity.

## 2.2 System Architecture (The "Sump-to-Sump" Loop)
*   **Suction Point:** Always taken from the **lowest point** of the engine main sump (where water and heavy sludge settle).
*   **Return Point:** Always returned to the **opposite end** of the sump, near the main LO pump suction, to ensure the entire oil volume is "turned over."
*   **Heater:** Uses steam or electric heaters to raise oil temp to 85°C – 95°C.

## 2.3 Operating Water System
A dedicated clean water supply (Fresh Water) is required to operate the bowl's hydraulic mechanism and create the water seal.

# Part 3 – Normal Operation & Key Parameters

## 3.1 What "Healthy" Looks Like
*   **Oil Clarity:** The oil should appear "bright and clear" (for MDO engines) or "dark but translucent" (for HFO engines).
*   **Continuous Operation:** Lube oil purifiers usually run 24/7, even when the engine is stopped, to keep the oil dry and warm.

## 3.2 Typical Operating Parameters

| Parameter | Healthy Value | Alarm / Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Separation Temp** | 85°C – 95°C | < 80°C (Poor Separation) |
| **Throughput (Flow)** | 10% – 20% of Capacity | > 25% (Additive Stripping) |
| **Discharge Interval** | 2 – 4 hours | > 8 hours (Disc Fouling) |
| **Water Content in Oil** | < 0.1% | > 0.5% (High Water Alarm) |
| **Sump Turnover Rate** | 3 – 5 times / 24h | < 2 times (Dirty Sump) |

# Part 4 – Common Faults & Root Causes

## 4.1 Emulsification (Water-in-Oil)
*   **Symptom:** Oil looks like "coffee with milk"; header tank level rises.
*   **Root Cause:** Major water leak in the engine (Jacket Water) or a loss of the purifier's water seal.
*   **Physics:** Once emulsified, the oil's surface tension changes, making centrifugal separation much harder.

## 4.2 Excessive Sludge Buildup
*   **Symptom:** Purifier vibrates; discharge cycle is very noisy; "Sludge Tank High" alarm.
*   **Root Cause:** High blow-by in the engine (worn rings) or poor combustion, leading to excessive soot in the oil.

## 4.3 Overflow (Oil Loss)
*   **Symptom:** LO sump level dropping rapidly; oil in the water outlet.
*   **Root Cause:** Incorrect gravity disc for the oil's specific gravity, or a failed "Closing Water" seal.

# Part 5 – Expert Troubleshooting & Trade Tricks

## 5.1 The "Steam Injection" Trick for Hard Sludge
*   **Trade Trick:** If the bowl is heavily fouled with hard carbon, some engineers "inject" a small amount of low-pressure steam into the oil inlet for 10 minutes before a discharge. The heat and moisture help soften the sludge so it "sloughs" off the discs easily.

## 5.2 Optimizing "Residence Time"
*   **Expert Insight:** Purification is a function of time. **Always run at the minimum flow rate** that still allows for 4 sump turnovers per day. Faster flow = less cleaning.

## 5.3 The "Quick Water Test" (The Hot Plate)
*   **Trade Trick:** To verify if the purifier is actually removing water, take a sample from the *Inlet* and *Outlet*. Drop them on a hot metal surface (150°C).
    *   **Inlet:** Should crackle/sputter (Water present).
    *   **Outlet:** Should smoke quietly (Dry oil).

## 5.4 Detecting "Additive Stripping"
*   **Expert Insight:** If your oil analysis shows a sudden drop in TBN but the oil is clean, you are likely running the purifier at too high a temperature (98°C+) or with too much "wash water." Reduce temp to 85°C to preserve additives.

# Part 6 – Maintenance & Preventive Checks

## 6.1 Daily Routine
*   **Vibration:** Feel the machine for "smoothness."
*   **Flow Check:** Ensure the flow meter is stable.
*   **Manual Discharge:** Perform one manual "Total Discharge" per day to ensure the bowl mechanism is not sticking.

## 6.2 The "Major Overhaul" (Every 8,000 Hours)
*   **Vertical Shaft:** Check for "run-out" (wobble) using a dial gauge. Max allowable is usually 0.05mm.
*   **Clutch Pads:** Ensure the machine reaches full speed within the manufacturer's time (e.g., 5-8 minutes).

## 6.3 Cleaning the Disc Stack
*   **Method:** Never scrape the discs with metal. Use a plastic scraper or a chemical soak. A single scratch on a disc provides a "anchor point" for hard sludge to grow.

# Part 7 – Miscellaneous Knowledge

*   **Detergent Oils:** Modern engine oils are "Detergent/Dispersant." They are designed to keep soot in suspension. The purifier must be powerful enough to "break" this suspension to remove the soot.
*   **System Warm-up:** Always keep the purifier running while in port. If the oil cools down, moisture will condense in the sump, leading to acid formation.
